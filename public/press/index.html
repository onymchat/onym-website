Boilerplate

One-liner

Onym is an open-source, end-to-end encrypted messenger anchored on Stellar -- no accounts, no phone numbers, no metadata trail.

50 words

Onym is an open-source messenger that replaces the trusted server with a public Stellar smart contract. Identity, keys, and message history live on the user's device. Group rules are anchored on chain, anyone can audit them, no one can override them. There are no accounts, no phone numbers, and no metadata trail to subpoena.

200 words

Onym is a privacy-first messenger built on a single principle: no one, including its makers, should be able to read your conversations or reconstruct your social graph. Where most messengers run on a trusted company's servers, Onym replaces the server with a public Stellar smart contract that defines a group's rules -- who can admit, who can remove, who can speak -- and a swarm of replaceable Nostr relays that carry encrypted blobs. Identity is twelve BIP-39 words generated and stored on the user's device; there are no accounts, phone numbers, or email addresses to compromise. Group membership is verified with zero-knowledge proofs (PLONK over BLS12-381) so admins can prove a user belongs without revealing the rest of the roster. Every cryptographic primitive in use -- BIP-39, secp256k1 / Schnorr, Ed25519, X25519, BLS12-381, PLONK, Poseidon -- has been studied, deployed, and attacked by cryptographers for decades. The iOS app, the Android app, the Stellar contracts, and the relayer are all MIT-licensed and on GitHub. Onym is built by a small team of independent cryptographers and engineers operating as the Onym Foundation.
